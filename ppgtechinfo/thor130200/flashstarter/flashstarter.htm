Polini flash starter failure

by Had Robinson

The problems

1. flash system

The OEM starter on the Thor 130 (and others) has various problems and a short life because of design and material issues.  Rather than have a durable starter (Top 80) and a decompression hole near the top of the cylinder (Simonini), Polini came up with a scheme made of mostly plastic parts that fails after a few dozen starts.  The intent, it seems, was to reduce the effort necessary to start the engine.  That any starter would have plastic parts is a mystery.  It is an example of poor engineering to save a little money.

When the mechanism starts to fail, parts of the flash mechanism begins to bulge out of the starter housing and prevents the tangs from engaging the ring on the flywheel.  Eventually, the entire starter comes apart or the mechanism gets caught in the flywheel and self-destructs.

In this photo, the plastic strip that permits the flash spring to wind and unwind smoothly has bunched up and has begun to push the flash spring out of its housing.  Did the engineers at Polini ever take a look at this Rube Goldberg mechanism?  It seems that they copied some of its parts....

2. weak mounting structure

Pilots in the normal course of starting the engine may also pull the lower starter mounting bolt right out.  Sometimes, the bolt will shear at the threaded end.

The mounting points for the flash starter

Below is a photo of the Polini flash starter mount reinforcement.  It helps prevent the twisting of the starter when it is pulled -- and the stripping/shearing of the existing bolts and mounting points.  However, it does nothing to prevent the disintegration of the flash mechanism.  This part does not seem to be available at this time in the western hemisphere.  If the lower mounting bolt is replaced with our special bolt and locknut, the starter should not come loose as easily.  So far, the modified flash starters on our engines have held steady without the addition of this reinforcing plate.

3. starter cord

Generally, the OEM starter cords are too thin and lack sufficient strength.  Please go to this page for the discussion and a source for a superior starter cord.

4. pulley failure

I do not know if other frame manufacturers (e.g. Parajet) use the same make and model of the Italian made starter pulley as Miniplane for their Polini engines.  Hopefully, the answer is "no".  The Miniplane pulley is of poor quality and design.  The OEM cord on the Polini starter can easily cut into the plastic of the rotating part of the pulley and render it useless.  The pulley uses nylon/plastic ball bearings which are guaranteed to fail after a short period.

Below is a photo of the OEM pulley that was quickly destroyed by the starter cord on a Thor 130.

Replacing a failed OEM pulley is useless as it will just fail again, especially if the OEM starter cord is not replaced with a stronger, thicker cord.  Determined pilots fed up with poor quality parts from Miniplane can purchase the Harken 082 Single Bullet Block (shown below).  It has a (5) year warranty and is superior in every way to the OEM block supplied by Miniplane.

 

Other than these annoying engineering problems, the Polini Thor engines are lightweight, quiet, and powerful -- something desirable.  In fact, the Polini's are among the quietest and smoothest engines on the market.

However, the flash starter problem makes these engines quickly unusable.

Solutions for these problems

We could bang our heads against a brick wall trying to convince the Italians to fix these problems but why waste the time?  Because we love these engines, even with these defects, we have come up with some fixes.

1. The insert for the flash mechanism

We have developed and manufactured a solid machined aluminum insert to replace the flash spring.  It is easy to install.  At the same time, the lower starter bolt must be replaced with a better quality bolt.  A locknut is needed to ensure that the bolt will not loosen.  With or without the modification, this bolt and/or the embedded nut in the cooling shroud, will eventually shear or the threads in the embedded nut will strip.

Why not replace the starter with the Polini non-flash type starter?  It is easy to do but the insert costs less.

However, when this modification is made, more strength is needed to start the engine.  Most pilots will have to place the motor on their backs and start it with both hands.  This is easy if the engine is primed correctly.  We have developed a technique that allows any engine with a diaphragm type carburetor to be started the first pull, every time.  No choke is used nor is it necessary.  It works when the engine is hot or cold.  It will not flood.

Warning  THE POLINI STARTER -- EVEN WITH THE MODIFICATION -- IS DELICATE.  DO NOT YANK REPEATEDLY ON THE STARTER!  THIS WILL DESTROY IT IN SHORT ORDER.

The kit includes the machined insert, a replacement bolt of better quality, and a locknut.  Pilots must have good mechanical skills to remove and disassemble the starter in order to replace the flash mechanism.

Please be sure follow these instructions for installing the flash starter replacement insert.

2. Improving the weak mounting structure

The most important modification is replacing the lower bolt with a tempered steel bolt and a locknut.  This is included in the insert kit from Miniplane-USA.

3. Heavy duty starter cord

Use a Dyneema cord.  It lasts forever and is designed for this sort of use.  It is available from Miniplane-USA.  Go here: http://www.miniplane-usa.com/parts/starter.htm and scroll down to "Top 80 Starter Cord, Dyneema 1.25m").  It will also work in other Polini Thor engines.

4. Better pulley

Harken pulleys are available everywhere in the world.  Sailrite sells the Harken 082 Single Bullet Block.  Miniplane-USA may also have them soon.
