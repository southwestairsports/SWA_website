Polini flash starter insert manual

Note:  Pilots who do not have good mechanical skills should not attempt this job.  Southwest Airsports can do it, if necessary.

Starter Parts

1 - main starter body with main spring and main insert
2 - flash starter spring
3 - pawl holder and screw
4 - pawl springs
5 - pawls
6 - flash starter insert (original)

Kit contents

(1) machined aluminum insert
(1) stainless steel screw 5mm x 0.8mm x 90mm and a 5mm locknut to replace the lower starter mounting screw

The replacement screw is necessary because Polini failed to account for the high stress put on the original screw when the starter rope is pulled.  The starter is, effectively, only attached with (3) screws because the top screw attachment point in the cooling shroud is -- in a word -- useless.  This is why the remaining (3) attachment points must have greater strength.  With the flash mechanism gone, the stress on the starter mounts is greater and pilots MUST be careful to NOT ever yank on the starter!

Assembly Instructions

1.  Remove the (2) 5mm bolts which attach the harness to the top of the frame.  Let the harness drop down enough to access the backrest attached to the frame.

2.  Remove the backrest (this exposes the front of the engine).  On some models, the backrest is attached with rivets which must be removed.  They can be replaced with screws, if desired.

3.  Detach the (2) starter rope pulleys from the frame.

4.  Remove the (4) starter mounting screws and the starter.  It is likely that the lower screw and/or nut insert is stripped.  If the screw is intact, keep it as a spare.

5. Disassemble the starter.

a.  Remove the screw and pawl cover (#3)
b.  Remove the pawls (#5) and springs (#4)
c.  Remove the flash insert (#6)
d.  Remove the flash spring (#2).  The spring is not under tension and can be safely removed.  DO NOT FURTHER DISASSEMBLE THE STARTER.

Here is the starter without the flash mechanism.  Clean all grease and debris out of the cavity that held the flash spring.

6. Assemble the starter with the flash insert replacement.  DO NOT USE ANY GREASE OR OIL WHEN REASSEMBLING THE STARTER.

a.  Place the aluminum insert into the cavity that held the flash spring.
b.  Place the springs on the roll pins in the aluminum insert
c.  Place the pawls over the pins and springs.
d.  Replace the pawl cover and tighten the screw.  Use blue threadlock.  Do NOT over-tighten the 5mm screw.  It just needs to be snug.

7.  Test the modified starter by pulling the cord a few times.  The pawls should smoothly move out from under the pawl cover as the cord is pulled and retract when the cord is released. 

8. Mount the starter on the engine.  Use blue threadlock on all screws.  Replace the lower screw with the longer one, installing it like the other screws.  If the nut insert is stripped, it does not matter.  Place the locknut on the end of the new screw which will firmly secure this mounting point.  Be EXTREMELY careful to NOT over tighten the mounting screws.  They should be just snug -- 4-6 Nm is plenty.

We have discovered that workers at the Polini factory routinely strip the starter screws and/or nuts during assembly so do not be surprised if HeliCoils are needed.  The are easy to install and are much stronger than the original.  I recommend that pilots do not bother to replace engine parts that have stripped threaded inserts/holes but, instead, always use HeliCoils, even if the engine is under warranty.  This is because they will just have the same problem again, sooner or later.  It is the price we pay for having one of the best paramotors on the market.  Hopefully, these annoyances will be fixed by Polini.  Meanwhile, consider how small the paramotor market is and how good the Polini is, otherwise.

9.  Test the starter on the engine.  I recommend getting into the same position relative to the paramotor as if flying.  Reach back and pull gently on the starter until resistance is felt.  Pull firmly with both hands to be sure that the starter is operating correctly.  If so, continue with the next step.

THE POLINI STARTER -- EVEN WITH THE MODIFICATION -- IS DELICATE.  DO NOT YANK REPEATEDLY ON THE STARTER!  THIS WILL DESTROY IT IN SHORT ORDER.

10.  Reassemble the back plate and harness.

11.  Prime the carburetor using this technique.  It is imperative that pilots ALWAYS use this method to start the engine as it will minimize yanking and repeated pulling on the starter mechanism and wearing it out.

Post Modification

Pilots must be careful not to pull too hard (yank) on the starter as it is not sturdy, despite the presence of the insert.  The engine has high compression and is much harder to pull over without the flash mechanism.  It is suggested that it only be started with the paramotor on the back using two hands.  For pilots of average strength and size, this is a much better and SAFER option.
