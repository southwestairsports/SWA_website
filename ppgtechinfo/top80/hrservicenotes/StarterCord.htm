Starter Replacement Cord

Miniplane USA has has a supply of 1.25m lengths of 3mm 100% Dyneema line that is imported from the U.K.  It is ordinarily used in the rigging of sailboats and is designed for extreme strength and durability as it must run through pulleys under great load.  It is more durable, thinner, and stronger than stock.  It is 0.25m longer than the original cord.  Taking the engine off the frame and disassembling the starter is not particularly easy and you want to do it as infrequently as possible.

Remember to thoroughly clean the insides of your starter if you are going to replace the cord.  It is better to use pure graphite powder to lubricate everything because it will not attract dirt.  This part of the engine is a powerful vacuum cleaner and the smallest amounts of dust will get mixed in with the stock grease that is used to lubricate the starter parts and gum everything up, especially the starter spring.  This greatly increases the wear on these parts.  Over the years, I have seen globs of grease on starters which do nothing but shorten their life.

Because it is thinner, the extra length will fit on the spool and pilots will not engage the end of the cord as easily when starting their engines.  Not pulling the cord to the very end saves a great amount of wear and tear on the starter assembly.  If you have worn out your stock cord, this is the cord to use as a replacement.
