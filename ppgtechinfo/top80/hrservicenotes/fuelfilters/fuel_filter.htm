Fuel Filters

by Had Robinson

If the best is desired, purchase the WIX #33001.  It has at least a 5X greater filtering area than the OEM filter from Miniplane.  Both are available from Miniplane-USA.    Do not be tempted to buy an inline fuel filter at an auto parts store because they are almost always of inferior quality.

Filter quality & capacity

Why bother with a high quality filter?  It must be able to trap all of the grit and other contaminants in the fuel and lubricating oil in order to prevent the carburetor from getting clogged.  A clogged carburetor cannot deliver the correct amount of fuel to the engine.

A CLOGGED FUEL SYSTEM IN ANY HIGH PERFORMANCE ENGINE IS A POTENTIAL DISASTER.  FUEL STARVATION IN THE TOP 80 IS THE #1 CAUSE OF ENGINE DAMAGE!

Aviation engine forums (ROTAX) note that paper filters are the best because they have the finest filtration media â€“ around 10 microns vs. 40+ micros for sintered bronze filters.  The latter do not trap the very small particles which will clog the fuel inlet screen and other tiny openings in the carburetor.  A clear filter housing must be used because pilots need to be able to periodically check that the filter is not clogged.

Here is a clogged fuel inlet screen from a Top 80 carburetor.  Instead of using a quality filter, the pilot used a sintered bronze filter which allowed contaminants to reach the carburetor.  This engine experienced fuel starvation and overheated.  Fortunately, it was not damaged because the lack of fuel was so severe the engine shut down before damage could occur.

Here is a sintered bronze filter.  The dirt-bike guys derisively refer to these as "lawnmower engine filters" because of their inability to filter well.

Fuel pump diaphragm from the carburetor above showing signs of water contamination in the fuel, probably from using ethanol blends.

Below is a photo of the WIX inline fuel filter (L) and a similar filter (R) sold by the auto parts stores.  Note that the size of the WIX filtering media is probably 10X that of the other.  The filter media of the WIX is 10 microns but who knows what it is of the filter on the right.  The absence of barbs on the hose connections guarantees that the filter on the right will leak at some point.  The barbs on the WIX also ensure that the filter will not become easily disconnected from the hoses -- which could be hazardous.  Why put a cheap fuel filter on a $3,000 engine?  Beats me....

Depending on use, fuel filters should be replaced yearly.  Ethanol and fuel that is contaminated by water hasten the deterioration of paper fuel filters.  The in-tank filter on the end of the fuel pickup hose should be checked to be sure it is not clogged.  Ethanol fuel blends can form gelatinous globs from contaminants which will quickly clog the tank filter, so be sure to check it often using the quick fuel system test.

Installation tips

To easily remove the fuel filter from the tubing, heat the tubing with a hot air gun or a hair dryer until it is almost too hot to touch.  The tubing will easily slip right off of the fittings without damaging anything.

When connecting the filter, put a small amount of pure silicone grease on the outlets and inside the tubing.  This will greatly ease the installation of the tubing.  It will also keep it from splitting.

If the WIX filter is used, its large size has to be considered.  The best location is just to the inside left of the right sidebar.  Secure it with a long nylon zip tie.  Use a piece of black electrical tape (not shown) to keep the tie from slipping up or down on the filter.
