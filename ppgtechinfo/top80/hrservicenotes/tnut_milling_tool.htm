T-Nut Milling Tool

Here is a short video on how to use it and a drawing from Miniplane with the milling specifications.

This tool from Miniplane is used to enlarge the stock holes in the hub and propeller so that 8mm T-nuts and bolts can be inserted.  A T-Nut with a bolt is far more resistant to shear failure than a simple nut with a bolt.  However, bolt failure is extremely rare.  I have never actually seen a propeller mounting bold shear or fracture.

Pilots must have a drill press, a depth gauge, and a caliper to do this modification.  For a modest charge, we can do this.  Please send us the propeller, hub, and the T-Nuts.

Incorrectly attaching the propeller, excessive run-out and/or out-of-track, damaged propellers, and propellers with incorrect mounting dimensions cause excessive vibration and may lead to the alleged propeller bolt failure.  The center hole on the propeller must FIRMLY fit onto the hub projection.  If it is not tight fit, either the hub or the propeller is damaged, usually the latter.  Upgrading to the super-strong T-Nuts is no substitute for not fixing what may  cause bolt failure in the first place.

All parts can be ordered from Miniplane-USA.
