Finger Screw Modification

by Had Robinson

Having to replace expensive screws in order to perform routine ignition, cooling fan, or starter service on the Top 80 is a poor option.  I have tried many ways to get an end-wrench or a crow-foot on them in order to properly torque them down on re-assembly but have not been successful.  The clearances are just too small.  The result will always be that the screws are not tight enough and the screws/pawl assembly will likely be bent/damaged.  It is easy to loosen the screws but it is impossible to reinstall them correctly so that the cooling fan will not move.

Damaged finger screw (hex bolt) from an attempt to re-use finger screws without modifying them.

A standard deep socket must be used when re-installing the screws.  The cooling fan spins at 9K+ RPM and the screws must be properly torqued.

Here is a permanent way to be able to re-use the screws.  Average mechanical skills and the knowledge of how to drill and tap a hole are needed.  Google the Internet for detailed instructions on how to do this.

Needed Items

Drill press, a Dremel type tool, and the following:

1. m3 x .5 tap set (taper, plug, and bottom taps)  These taps are small and delicate so be careful when using them.

2. #44 drill bit or the metric equivalent

3. (3) m3 x 8mm screws

Begin by using the Dremel to grind away the peened tops of the screws down to the washer.  Do not grind the washer!  Remove the washers by grabbing them with a pair of pliers and rocking them off the tops of the screws.  They will be re-used.  Remove the pawls and the springs.  If you are not removing the cooling fan, just loosen (but don't remove) the bolts.  Remove the screws one at a time to do the mod.

If the all of the screws are removed at the same time, the spacers between the cooling fan and the flywheel (on some Top 80 models) may get out of alignment.  If that happens, it will be difficult getting them all back in the right position without taking off the cooling box.  The engine must be in the horizontal position on these models or the spacers under the cooling fan will slide around.  New models of the Top 80 have the spacers attached to the cooling fan.

Fortunately, the finger screws are cone shaped at the pawl end so it is not difficult to center drill the hole for the new screw.

1.  Make sure the washers will fit over the end of the bolt and rest on the shoulder of the machined end.  You might have to take a small file to clean up the ends so that the washers go on and off easily.

2.  With the drill press, drill a hole in the end of the screw about 10mm deep.  Thread the hole (be sure to use cutting fluid). Clean with brake-cleaner & air.  The modified finger screws below can be re-used for the life of the engine.



If you do it right, the screw will bottom in the hole and just snug the washer onto the shoulder near the end of the bolt.  Be certain to use blue thread-lock on the screws.  Reassemble the starter and test.  Below is the finger screw, starter pawl, and spring detail.

That's it!
