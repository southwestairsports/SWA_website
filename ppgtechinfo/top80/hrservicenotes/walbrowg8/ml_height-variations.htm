Metering lever height variation in the Walbro WG-8

by Had Robinson

Over the years, I have noted that Miniplane ships out new Top 80 engines with the metering lever (ML) height on the carburetor set to a value different than given in their service manual.  Extensive tests have demonstrated that Miniplane's Italian service manual value is correct rather than the value measured in carburetors on new engines from the factory.

Why is this?  I am not sure.  Dave Jewell of blue Sky PPG suggests that Miniplane may have failed to update the specifications when they changed carburetors some years ago.  For a long time, I set ML's to the same value as on new engines.  This was a mistake, as I now realize.  The problems we experienced were poor throttle response and occasional stalling when suddenly going to full power.  In addition, leaning out the high speed mixture can quickly overheat the engine and cause permanent damage.

The measured value on new WG-8's has consistently been 1.7mm.  This is also the same measured value of ML's in the rebuild kits from Walbro.  This high value prevents the inlet needle valve from opening fully (only about 0.5mm).  At the lower value specified in the Italian Miniplane service manual, the valve will open 1mm.  This is a significant difference â€“ 100% greater.

I tested a newly rebuilt engine with both values.  With the high value, sudden operation of the throttle almost always stalled the engine (it was briefly starved of fuel).  With the low value, I could hit the throttle to fully open from idle and the engine would immediately accelerate to full speed without sputtering or stalling.  It also seemed that the mid range was also smoother.  I wondered whether running at full load for extended periods might burn up the engine if the higher value (1.7mm) was used?  This is not something I wanted to find out.

Obviously, engines with the higher ML value will run but may fade when going to full power or have poorer throttle response.  There is also the risk of leaning out the engine (burning it up).
