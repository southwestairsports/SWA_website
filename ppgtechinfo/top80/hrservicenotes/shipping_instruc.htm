Service, repair, and shipping instructions

Do not ship anything to us without prior authorization!

1.Cost

Estimates and labor are charged at $95/hour with a (1) hour minimum charge.  You must pay for shipping and insurance both ways.

Note: I travel extensively in order to train pilots, fly in airshows, and develop paragliding sites.  I work on equipment when I have the time to do it and I am at the shop.  Please be patient.  If it is urgent, let me know.  I may have to charge extra for rush jobs.  Thanks, -Had

2. What to send

If you are not sure what service your equipment needs or you want me to test fly your equipment, send the entire engine with all accessories.  If it is any paramotor other than a Top 80 or Polini, you must send everything, including the frame.

If the propeller must be sent (and is a single piece), it is better to ship it in a separate box.  Be sure to protect the tips from damage during shipment!

3. Form to complete

Please print, fill out, and enclose this information worksheet with your equipment.  Do not use pencil.  Write neatly and carefully.

4. How to pack -- tote container REQUIRED for engines

If you are sending an engine, you must purchase a plastic tote from Lowes or Home depot like this one for about $14.  However, if you have your ORIGINAL box that your engine was shipped in and it is in good condition, you may ship it in that.  Carefully pack your engine for shipment. Remove the spark plug and airbox.  Please detach all fuel hoses, the choke, and throttle cables so they are not damaged in shipment.  The carburetor and starter are the more fragile parts of the engine and must be carefully padded.

If we get an engine not shipped in a tote or in the original box, we will buy one for you but it will cost more.

If you are shipping an entire motor, do not detach the muffler.  The muffler helps protect the engine from damage.  The equipment should be tightly packed in and surrounded with 1" foam board or the equivalent so that it CANNOT MOVE inside the tote.  Used carpet and/or padding may also be used to thoroughly wrap things and is easier to use than stiff foam board.  If needed, use foam peanuts, empty water bottles to ensure that things will not move inside the tote.  A fuel tank can be used as padding if it is protected from puncture damage from a screw or sharp edge.  The packed equipment must be able to survive a drop of 3' from any position.  Use nylon ties to secure the lid to the container.

5. Who to ship it with

Send via UPS or FEDEX ground INSURED for full value.  We have had equipment destroyed by these shippers, so they must be insured.

6. Payment

We accept checks, money orders, and credit cards (given over the phone).  If payment is made by credit card, please add 3%, the fee charged us by the provider.

7. Post service

If we are servicing/repairing an entire engine, it will tuned to run at our altitude (4,000' MSL).  If you are at a different altitude above sea level, you MUST learn how to adjust your carburetor.  Humidity, temperature, and altitude ALL affect how the engine runs so all pilots must be familiar with how to do this.
