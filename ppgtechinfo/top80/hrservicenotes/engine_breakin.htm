Engine Break-In

by Had Robinson

Proper break-in will provide you with a better running engine and considerably longer engine life.  Most engines are not properly broken in.  It takes time.  Remember that running at full throttle on a new engine for more than brief periods should not be done.

The break-in procedure can be done with the paramotor on your back.  It is a GOOD time for you to become more familiar with the feel and sound of your paramotor.  While doing the break-in fiddle with items around you like the buckles, the choke, etc.  The more the throttle is second nature, the safer pilot you will be.

Do not increase the amount of oil in the fuel during the break-in period.  As Polini and other engine manufacturers note in their user manuals, it does nothing to improve the lubrication of the engine.  Instead, it will cause the engine to run hotter (leaner), even causing it to seize.

A new engine generates a lot more friction (heat) because the moving parts of the engine have tighter clearances. It can more easily seize than an engine with many hours.  The break-in procedure allows the space between moving parts to increase slightly to the designed value.  If your engine suddenly starts to lose power, it is probably overheating.  Immediately back off on the throttle to idle.  This break-in procedure is a little more detailed than the official Miniplane procedure and can be used with other engines. 
.
The procedure listed below takes less than 30 minutes and is done on the ground.  After this is done, you can fly.

Put the engine on your back and take a walk.  Not only will you break the engine in but you will get used to it being on your back.  If you have a CHT installed, keep the temperature well below the specified maximums.

  1. Start the engine and let it idle until it is up to normal running temperature (cooling fins too hot to touch).  Idle it for another (10) minutes.
  2. Vary the RPM to 1/2 throttle and back to idle for 2 minutes.  Stop the engine and let it cool off for 2 minutes.
  3. Start the engine.  Vary the RPM from idle to 1/2 throttle and back for 5 minutes.  Stop the engine and let it cool off for 5 minutes.
  4. Start the engine.  Vary the RPM from idle to full throttle (briefly) for 10 minutes.  Stop the engine and let it cool off for 5 minutes.
  5. Continue 10 minutes of running, 5 minutes of cooling until 30 minutes of total run time is achieved.

At this point, you can fly.  Avoid full power for more than (5) minutes and vary the throttle often during the break-in period.

Break-in is complete after a full tank (10 liters/2.6 gallons) of fuel have been run through the engine.  Polini and Minari note that break-in is complete after two tanks (20 liters) of fuel has been consumed.  If it were my engine, I would be really easy on it until (20) liters of fuel have been consumed.

All engines should have the head bolts/nuts checked after the first (10) hours of use.  For the proper torque value, check the respective specification page of your engine.  The Top 80 value is 9 Nm.
