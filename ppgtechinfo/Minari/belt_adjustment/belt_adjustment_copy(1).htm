Belt adjustment on the Minari and others

by Had Robinson

Note:  As of August 3, 2016, I am working on a much simpler, easier way to properly adjust these belts.  The only tools required are a simple 3/8" click-type torque wrench, a 3/8" drive metric hex bit set, and a hand-type metric hex bit set.  I'll get it here as soon as possible.  So far, it keeps the belt tension reasonably within specifications.

The re-drive belt adjustment is similar on all paramotors.  You know your belt is loose if the paramotor "chirps" at idle.  This noise is caused by the sudden slipping of the engine pulley and the belt.  If the belt is loose, the paramotor will not achieve full thrust and the belt will wear out more quickly due to friction.  If the belt is too tight, it will cause the redrive bearing to heat up, including the belt, and this can cause both to fail.

Always give the belt a quick check to be sure it is not frayed or about to fail.  A belt failure is a disaster and will result in the engine quickly over-revving.  This will cause the engine to self-destruct.

DO NOT USE BELT DRESSING ON THE RE-DRIVE BELT.

In the steps below, refer to the the Minari engine diagram for parts identification.  You will need a 6mm hex bit to loosen the clamping screw and the shaft concentric.

1. Loosen the the redrive shaft clamping screw (#26) just enough so that you can easily turn the re-drive shaft concentric (#82).

2. Turn the re-drive shaft concentric one way or the other with the hex bit in order to adjust the belt tension.  You should be able to twist the belt no more than 40 degrees.

3. Retighten the clamping screw 9-10 Nm to prevent the shaft concentric from turning.
