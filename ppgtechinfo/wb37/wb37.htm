Walbro WB-37 carburetor information

The parts kit needed is the K10-WB.

Rather than a fixed high speed jet like the WG-8, this carburetor has an adjustable high speed jet.  It is serviced the same as the WG-8 EXCEPT for the high speed adjustment.  Be EXTREMELY careful adjusting the high speed system and only follow your paramotor manufacturer's instructions (if any).  It is easy to BURN UP your engine!  Generally, if you do not have a CHT (at the minimum), do not change the high speed adjustment from the factory defaults.

The Minari has instructions on how to adjust the WB-37 on the 180cc engine but it is only for that engine and those flying at sea level.  If you have another Minari engine or fly at higher altitudes, follow the instruction on this page.

Basic carburetor setup

Use the steps below to adjust your carburetor if your user manual has no information OR you operate your engine at high altitude.

BE EXTREMELY CAREFUL THAT THE CYLINDER HEAD TEMPERATURE (CHT) NEVER EXCEEDS 200-220ºC (392-428ºF)!

It is always safer to run an engine a bit rich (so it runs cooler) than lean (so it runs hotter).  There is a 30-60 second lag in the CHT gauge so always throttle up slowly, watching the temperature at all times.  It is very easy to quickly overheat an engine by running it lean.  If you find that the instructions below result in your engine overheating, simply enrich the high speed mixture.  Turn the high speed jet (#1 in the photo below) counter-clockwise 1/8th of a turn then check the temperature.  Continue to turn it 1/8th of a turn counter-clockwise until a safe running temperature is achieved.

1. Open screw #1 (1.5) turns from fully closed as a basic setting.  Start the engine and let it warm up.

2. Turn screw #3 in or out until the idle speed is 1,900 RPM to 2,100 RPM.

The next step is potentially very dangerous!  You must secure the engine to some immovable object like a tree, car hitch, or telephone pole/post.

3. Go to full throttle and close screw #1 until there is a slight decrease in RPM.  The engine is now running in a slightly lean condition at full throttle.

4. Release the throttle and open screw #1 (3/4) of a turn.  This must be done carefully.  You do not want the engine to exceed the maximum temperature given above.

5. With engine idling, open/close screw #2 until maximum speed is obtained.  Suddenly apply full throttle.

If the engine begins to stall, open #2 screw (1/16) of a turn.  Suddenly apply full throttle again and see if the engine begins to stall.  If it does, open screw #2 another (1/16) of a turn.  Continue this step until the engine no longer stalls when full throttle is suddenly applied.

If the engine does NOT begin to stall, close screw #2  (1/16) of a turn.  Suddenly apply full throttle.  If the engine does not stall, continue to close screw #2 in (1/16) increments until the engine begins to stall when full throttle is applied.  If the engine begins to stall, back out the screw (1/16) turn.  This is the correct adjustment of the low speed system.

You may have to re-adjust screw #3 in order to achieve the correct idle speed.  In the photo below screw #1 is the high speed jet screw adjustment.  #2 is the low speed jet adjustment.  #3 is the idle speed adjustment.
