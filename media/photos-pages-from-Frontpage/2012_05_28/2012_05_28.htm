06/06/16

 

May 28 Monday – Memorial Day – Our thanks to our soldiers everywhere who honor our nation with loyal service doing that which few would do but must be done.

While visiting Marilyn Robinson's mom (and widow of a D-Day survivor) I took some time to fly northeastern Oklahoma, a beautiful and verdant area of the country.  There are no high enough mountain launch sites in this region so paraglider pilots must be towed into the air or use power.  I chose to fly a foot-launched powered paraglider.

Below, the countryside east of Tahlequah, OK.  This small town is in the foothills just west of the Ozarks in Arkansas.

To the left is the upper end of the Lake Tenkiller.  The dam is over 30 miles SSE of here.

The arrow points to where the pilot made a brief landing.  The view is east and the winds were about 4 mph from the south so you could both land and then launch into the wind.  It is always a good skill to land and then launch in a strange place.

A close up of the landing area (arrow above) on the edge of the Lake.

A view of the LZ by the Lake.  It was a challenge finding some place that was not covered with trees.

Across from the LZ was a public landing for boats at the northern end of the Lake.

The far western end of Highway 100 just before it joins Highway 82 and then crosses the Lake (not visible but to the right).  A marina can be seen to the left of the bridge.

Cherokee Landing State Park.  This little peninsula into the Lake is used for launching boats and camping.

You must always look for and study possible landing sites in this heavily wooded region. (Strange they have so many trees, isn't it?)  This one is at the top of a ridge east of the Lake near Highway 82.

Below is the retirement center of Marilyn Robinson's mom in Tahlequah, OK.  Our RV is just visible in the left center of the photo.  The mowed grass everywhere provides an ideal site to launch/land for foot-launched powered paragliding.

Hope you enjoyed the show at the western edge of the Ozark mountains!
