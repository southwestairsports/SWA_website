 Valle de Bravo, Mexico - December 5-12, 2010 Part II (for Part I go here)

Our group in Valle: John Cummings, Rena Scott, Had Robinson, and Michael Pesek.  We are enjoying breakfast at one of our favorite spots early in the morning.  The coffee is generally "instant" in much of Mexico -- you have to hunt out the rare places that know how to brew fresh coffee.  Good wine is rarely available.  However, the fruit, vegetables, and fresh breads and tortillas are without rival, especially the juices.

The luxurious inner garden of our hotel, Casa Abierta

Had Robinson flying low at Las Penitas.  Rena Scott took this from the ground.  Never give up trying to get back up -- if you can do ti safely.

 

Had stayed up another 5 minutes but conditions were weakening and a bunch of other pilots followed him down  -- they can be seen in the photo here.

The cow (& pig ) pasture below Las Penitas

More gliders coming in -- every pilot struggled valiantly to stay up -- we still have to smile after any flight!

This hot dog pilot came in doing wingovers and other less than safe maneuvers near the ground.  He hit the top of a tree and then almost ran into this building near the LZ.  His wing got tangled in the rebar at the top.

Pilot Mike Pesek near launch (middle left)

View from launch

Pilot Rena Scott below Las Penitas -- she also sank out that day and had to land.

A wild orchid growing in the trees near Las Penitas

Had with a friend in the Las Penitas cow pasture

Cross country view looking north to the lake at Valle de Bravo.  Directly below is a beautiful hacienda in the middle of the forest.  Pilots must not land there -- it has guards and dogs.  Better to land in a tree, if you have to....

Rooftop setting at our hotel

The main plaza in Valle de Bravo.  As it was near Christmas, they have manger scenes and the like.  If you don't like the Christian faith, move to the U.S.

Packing crews at the LZ.  A local woman even has cold beer and snacks for sale.

Had landed at one of the many bailouts near El Peñon.  He had grabbed a nearby thermal but it was weakening and drifting up the canyon to the right -- a dangerous area with no place to land so he flew out of the thermal, back down the canyon, and landed here.

View of El Peñon from the LZ.  The Wall is just behind -- it is here that you can really get altitude if the conditions are good.
