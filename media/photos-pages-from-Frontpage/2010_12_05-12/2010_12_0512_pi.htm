 Valle de Bravo, Mexico - December 5-12, 2010 Part I (for part II go here)

December 6 -12 Valle de Bravo, Mexico - Where can you fly every day with great thermalling conditions, in a beautiful mountain setting, and land right at your favorite lunch hangout, then rest, and go flying again in the afternoon?  Valle is the place!  Here is a sample from our latest trip to this special region of the world for hang gliding and paragliding.

This year's pilots in Valle de Bravo (L-R): Had Robinson, Rena Scott, Jeff Hunt (our host), John Cummings, Michael Pesek.

Below is the view just off launch towards the LZ which is the open area just to the upper left of the boot in the photo.

Near cloud base looking north towards the lake at Valle de Bravo - I (Had) was doing some cross country here.

This is the inside courtyard of our hotel -- Casa Abierto

We fly across the countryside and land at a field right next to this restaurant called "Jovan's - Cocina Economica" (cheap restaurant).  However, the food is some of the best I have ever had in Mexico.  The proprietress is standing to the left in the photo below.  The food is cooked right under your eyes....

The Peñon -- this is a goal pilots need to go to from launch.  "The Wall" is right behind it.

Well, are we happy or not???  The camera on the helmet is a GoPro HD video camcorder.  It takes amazing video at 60 frames per second - wide screen.

Looks like an African tree?  It's a landmark at the main landing zone for the area.
