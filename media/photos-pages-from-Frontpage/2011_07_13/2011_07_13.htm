06/06/16

 

July 13 – East Potrillo Mountains –   Lee Boone & Had Robinson made the trip out to our new PPG launch area 3 miles southeast of the southern tip of the East Potrillo Mountains.  Because it is monsoon season, the atmosphere is amazingly buoyant and the air is relaxed.  We had about 2 hours in the air traveling up the entire spine of the E. Potrillo Mountains and enjoying the occasional ridge soaring there.  We landed just south of Mt. Riley and then re-launched to explore Mt. Cox.  The air can be unpredictable so we were careful.  While the balloon soundings indicated SSE and WSW winds all the way up, the air above Mt. Cox was from the NW.  Go figure....  Nonetheless, we could ridge soar the NW face of Mt. Cox.  We had planned to top land on Mt. Riley but the NW winds would make the air turbulent up there.

Below is our new launch area near the Potrillo Mountains (in the background).  It is accessible from Hwy 9, flat, and has plenty of launch space even for trikes.  The next three frames show Lee's launch.  the winds were about 6 from the SSW.

Just after we launched, we flew to the mountains just off the deck – like the cruise missile.

This is the highest point of the E. Potrillo Mountains.  Plenty of ridge lift around here.  However, because of the little ridges coming out, this area would not be good for HG/PG ridge soaring as when the air gets stronger, it gets a lot more turbulent here.

Note the caves near the top of the peak.  We actually got quite close to them.  They are not visible from the ground so a trip is demanded to see if they were once inhabited by the locals.

Mt. Riley from the south.  I think it is evident that when the air is steady, this mountains provides smooth lift.

Neighboring Mt. Cox looking northwest.

Cruising along with Lee Boone.

Local Ace Pilot Lee Boone ridge soaring the NW face of Mt. Cox.  Lift was good but it was getting late and we could not stick around.
