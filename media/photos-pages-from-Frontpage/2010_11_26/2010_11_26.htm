 Mt Riley "Hike 'n Fly"

Friday November 26 - CONGRATS TO DENAE NEMANIC FOR HER FIRST MOUNTAIN FLIGHT!  Andy Gonzalez (Albuquerque), Denae Nemanic, and Had Robinson made the trek up Mt. Riley in the AM arriving at the summit about 11:30AM.  Conditions over the region were very mild with light winds out of the NW and W.  Andy launched first and flew out, Denae soared and thermaled west of the peak under direction of her instructor, Had Robinson.  The last to leave, Had launched, flew out and landed near the upper truck parking area so the pilots would not have to make the hike up from the LZ (about a mile).  Here are some photos.

Denae & Andy at the upper parking area west of Mt Riley. Riley is "hike 'n fly" -- a rugged 1,000 vertical feet to the summit.

Andy setting up.

Denae ready to launch to the west.  It was cold up there!  The West Potrillo Mountains are visible just above her head.  Mt No-Name is to the right and the Florida Mountains of Deming, New Mexico are visible in the far upper right.

Denae heading out due west.  To the left is Mt Cox.  The West Potrillo mountains are in the background.  You can see that in this photo, Denae is above launch.  The air was buoyant out front near the summit.  She stayed high for some time before flying over to the south face of the peak.

In the photo below, Denae is just about to encounter a powerfulthermal over that small hill.  Had she turned right instead of left, she could have gone up to base that day -- 9,000'.  Thermals down low are narrow and sharp edged and there is not much room for error.  Furthermore, they only get strong enough to take the pilot up about every 10-15 minutes.  It takes time for the thermal to develop near the ground and then when it's ready to shoot up (like an Aladdin lamp), the pilot better be there ready to take it.  The LZ is just behind her, on the other side of the arroyo in the distance.
