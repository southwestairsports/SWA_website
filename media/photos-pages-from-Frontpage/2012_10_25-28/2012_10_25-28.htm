October 25 - 28 Fiesta del Globo  and Aeroshow Xtremo – Chihuahua City, Chih., Mexico –   Our own El Paso Paramotor Demo Team was able to perform at this popular event that drew visitors from all over northern Mexico.  The event was hosted by the State of Chihuahua and various sponsors including the Hacienda de Chihuahua, a sotol distillery in northern Mexico.  Paramotor pilot Jose Muñoz, also of Chihuahua City, was our liaison during the show.  Paramotor pilots attending from the U.S. were Zak Hargraves, Matt Watson, and Had Robinson.

FlyersTeam provided most of the performances of fixed wing aircraft.  They also graciously invited our small paramotor group to share their space at the Fiesta.

Our ground crew at the Fiesta (L-R):  David Villegas, Jesús Lopez Perez, Omar Ruiz, and Diana Cano.  They provided so much help for the pilots that allowed us to fly and not exhaust ourselves setting up, carrying equipment, and helping fend off curious spectators.  THANK YOU SO MUCH, GUYS!

The pilots (L-R):  Jose Muñoz, Had Robinson (EP Paramotor Demo Team), Zak Hargraves, and Matt Watson

The Fiesta location at the La Presa reservoir southwest of the city of Chihuahua.  It is early morning and the balloons have just begun launching.  Behind the reservoir is La Salle University.  The city has many universities and some of the best restaurants in North America.

Mexican air shows are more like they used to be in the U.S.  Operations are done close – and often – just over spectators.

This photo was hard to take.  The aircraft is not 50' off the deck doing 175 mph right over show central.

Our host, Fiesta del Globo, gave us a welcome feast our first evening at our motel, the luxurious Holiday Inn & Suites of Chihuahua.  All pilots had private rooms in this clean and quiet motel near the north end of the city.  Fiesta del Globo paid for all of our expenses, including meals, while we were in Cd. Chih.  Thank you very much!

Ready to deploy the streamer 1,000' above the ground

Off it goes – the pilot must not let go of the bridal or he will lose the streamer!

Once the d-bag starts to drop and the streamer begins to come out, the bridal must be put on the foot quickly.

The d-bag is falling nicely towards the earth.  If the pilot is too low, it can hit the ground.  This is not a problem if the area is clear and there is nothing that can snag the streamer.  It is always best to drop it over water as it cannot snag on anything, it can only get wet – but that presents other challenges for the pilot that can be overcome.

Letting down the 700' streamer into the lake far below

Though hard to see, the streamer has entered the water far below.  Once the end gets wet, it gets heavy.  How does the pilot pull it out without breaking the weak link?  If he ascends slowly in a circle, the streamer will be gently pulled out of the water and weak link will not break.

The crowds love the streamer as it is graceful and beautiful to watch.  It was a great show at Fiesta del Globo 2012.
