06/06/16

 

June 19 – Paragliding on the Moon – Glenn Tupper & Had Robinson went to the crest of the W. Potrillo Mountains today.  Here is an amazing YouTube Glenn made of this trip.  Below are a few photos of the trip.

Looking west above Hwy 9 at the south end of the E. Potrillo Mountains.  Glenn is seen here doing a long launch run on the lonely highway.  Both pilots were flying foot-launched powered paragliders which always require a run to launch - sometimes a few steps (in stronger air) or a 50 yard marathon, as in the photo below, when the air is still or the pilot finds he is launching downwind.  Glenn had to run from about 25 yards below the bottom of the photo.  Most pilots would abort the launch but he was determined!

The beginning of the 15 mile cruise across the desert just 20' off the deck – the way to cross the desert.

Entering the moonscape of the West Potrillo Mountains.

View of the region's highest peak, Mt. Cox, from the south.

Looking southeast and back to launch from about half way to the Moon.  Launch is about 2 miles beyond the southern tip of the East Potrillo mountains pictured here.

Glenn Tupper took all of the following photos.  One of the bigger volcanic cones in the W. Potrillo Mountains. 

The view west from the crest of the range.

The scattered volcanic cones near the center of the range. The view is north here.

Looking south from the center of the range.

The trip back.  In the distance are (R-L): Mt. Cox, Mt. Riley, and Mt. No-Name.

Glenn took this amazing moonscape photo looking down on Had Robinson.

Cruising by the southwest face of Mt. Cox.

The West Potrillo's viewed from Mt. Cox.  The shadow of the peak is visible in the lower part of the photo.
