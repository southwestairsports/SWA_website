06/06/16

 

 July 12 - 18 Anapra Mesa On!  – It was a dizzying amount of air time at Anapra Mesa the other week.  Thanks to La Nina and endless east winds, Anapra was probably flown more in these few days than in recent years.  We were all flying so much, posting our adventure was not possible.

New PPG pilot, Bobby Dockins, looks on as Lee Boone ridge soars the Anapra Mesa.

Lee preparing to launch.  Bobby is assisting.  Anapra has a barb wire fence just behind launch so help during launch is always advisable.

Looking south from high and in front of the mesa.  The pilot here is in a standing wave caused by the mountains in front of the mesa. If the mesa were isolated, it would never be possible to get this far out in front of such a small ridge.

Soaring with the birds (a vulture) – our often companions in the air.  They know where the air is going up!

High up and in front of the mesa – the city of Juarez, Mexico is visible in the background.

Looking west – this pilot had never been this high and as far in front of the mesa than here – thanks to the "Second Wave" at Anapra Mesa.

The view east – the pilot here is in Mexican airspace.  The border fence is visible below.  It is legal for us to do this but do not land on the other side of the border.  It is getting late in the day – almost sunset.

Hope you enjoyed the show!
