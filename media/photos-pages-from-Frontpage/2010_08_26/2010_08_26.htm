Lee Boone breaks PPG altitude record for our region > 14,000' MSL

Thursday - August 26, 2010

Ace pilot Lee Boone smoked all PG altitude records in our region by going to 14,100' MSL in his 80cc Miniplane paramotor.  "It was cold up there - just 35°F!" Lee quipped.  This is two vertical miles above the ground here and 2,000' above the highest peak in this region of the U.S. Lee landed safely from where he had launched, consuming just 6 liters of fuel (1/2 tank).

Here is an overall view of his flight path.  He launched from northeast El Paso (far right in the image) with his Miniplane and began the hunt for a thermal.  Finding one at about 700' AGL, he hooked it and began his climb out to over 10,000' where the thermal hit an inversion - everything stopped.

Note the tight circles as Lee stays in the thermal - which drifted westward due to an east wind.  The wind "pushed" the thermal over.

It was very cold at 14,100' - about 35 degrees.  Lee watched as jetliners came into El Paso beneath him....

Congratulations to Lee Boone for his fine demonstration of skill!
