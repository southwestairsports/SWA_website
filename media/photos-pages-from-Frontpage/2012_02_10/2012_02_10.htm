06/06/16

 

February 10 Friday – Salton City, CA – The annual Salton Sea fly-in is held on private property on the west shore of the little town of Salton City – a defunct California real estate development.  Its sparse population makes it ideal for powered paragliding = there are not touchy homeowners who might object to the annoying buzz of a dozens of paramotors flying overhead.  There are about (40) registered pilots participating from all over the States.  El Paso pilots include Oren & Erez Belkin and Had Robinson.  The presence of a large body of water gives the area the "lake effect".  Translated, this means that during the day, the air over the lake is cooled enough so that the surrounding desert air (which is heated by the sun) rises up and is replaced by the cool air over the Sea.  This means light onshore winds and – to the delight of PPG pilots – no thermals and laminar air.  At night, the winds aloft take over and the winds reverse.  The downside is that the sea is not quite salty enough to be sterile and the Sea can have strange odors (which is possibly why the shore of the lake and inland is sparsely populated.  But PPG pilots don't care!  The event has nightly parties and competitions (races, slaloms, and "kiting wars".  It also has its share of bizarre accidents (no injuries, thankfully).  Every skill level is represented (which makes it almost more interesting to watch the launch area than fly).
