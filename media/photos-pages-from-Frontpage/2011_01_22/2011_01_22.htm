 Agave Hill - January 22, 2011 Saturday - Flying at Dusk

Denae Nemanic, Tobias S., and Had Robinson had nothing better to do after flight school so we hiked up to Agave Hill to see how the winds would go as they were gusty and high all afternoon but there often is a break right at dusk.  Sure enough, about 30 minutes before sundown conditions started to abate. We quickly setup.  Tobias went first and then Denae (the air was going to zilch by now). Finally Had launched and enjoyed landing downwind in the katabatic flow which was starting. At least we all flew! It was a very narrow window that pilots have before things go to zero as the inversion forms over our region. Tobias made the main road nicely in his new Buzz III.

Denae & Tobias at the upper parking lot, Franklin Mountains State Park

Agave Hill launch.  Mundy's Gap is in the background to the right.

Tobias prepares to launch in the twilight as Denae looks on.

Who could this be?  When you land after sunset and when all lift has gone away, you sometimes have to put up with this sort of thing.  It wasn't too bad – 15 minutes and the lines were out of the ocotillo.  It is always good to fly!  Hang gliders don't have this problem.  However, they have wing tips low to the ground that can snag this stuff as they come in to land.  Every tijme we launch and land, no matter how short the flight, it is a valuable experience for pilots.

We had a blast – please join us next time!  (Tobias S., Denae Nemanic, and Had Robinson).
