06/06/16

 

February 29 Wednesday - Kilbourne Hole Maar Epic - Air coming in was about 220 degrees at 15-18 mph, sometimes surging even higher.  Pilots Lee Boone & Had Robinson needed to fly! The winds were on the edge.  The Jet was overhead but we figured that this volcanic monster was low enough that Jet turbulence would be minimal.  The beauty of flying in a crater is that you start out near ground level and fly over a giant hole in the ground 2 miles across.  Lee was the first to go up from the west facing main launch.  He was able to soar the SSW facing part of the launch area – and go high, higher than he ever had before.  Had launched next about 1/4 north of the main launch but the flight was short because the winds were turning more south.  He launched again from the main launch and went up – and up and up – higher than he had ever been at this site, reaching an AGL of over 600'.  At this height he flew up wind to get out of the lift.  Who knows how much higher it went?  Both pilots had some tip folds and other anomalies for air so late in the day so we decided it was best to come down.  Flying when the Jet is overhead is always dicey.  We probably should not have been in the air this day – but the flight at Kilbourne was EPIC.

Lee at the main launch waiting for a slight lull in the high winds.  The launch area is so clean because of the winds that sometimes roar through here....

Lee parked above and south of the main launch.  In the far distance are the mountains west of Juarez, Mexico.  The end of the Franklin Mountains is just visible in the lower left.

Had at 600' AGL!  The dark rock is the layer of volcanic basalt that was thrown up when the crater exploded (this is one of few flyable maars in the world).   He is twice the height of the rim to the bottom of the crater.  How so high?  He is not flying backwards so it must be some sort of wave lift added to the ridge at the rim.  This was a record for Kilbourne Hole Maar.  In the distance to the right are the East Potrillo Mountains.

Lee is parked above the east launch.  How fun is this!

More parking above Kilbourne Hole Maar.  The air was so strong we could stand still above the ground, as here.

Lee top landed just SE of launch.  If you land at the bottom of the Hole, it's a 40 minute hike back out.  In the far distance are the Franklin Mountains.

Hope you enjoyed the show!
