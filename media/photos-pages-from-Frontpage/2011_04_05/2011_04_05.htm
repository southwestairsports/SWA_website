06/06/16

 

Tuesday April 5 – Flying Dry Canyon, Alamogordo, NM – Visiting hang gliding pilots David Daws and Jay Fisher from the Austin area joined local PG pilots Lee Boone and Had Robinson for some great AM flying at Dry Canyon.  Conditions were great for HG and on the strong side for PG because of the strong winds that quickly built up in the morning.  Lee and Had landed just in time before the winds started to really speed up.

Moments after launch, this view presents itself to those lucky enough to fly Dry!  The White Sands are visible in the distance and north Alamogordo is in the foreground.  The air is blue and dry at 7,500'.

David and Jay setting up their gliders at launch.

Jay Fisher comes in for a perfect landing on the huge Cox LZ.  David Daws just didn't want to come down so we were unable get a photo of him!

Lee Boone on approach to the LZ.  Note that he is standing up in the harness, ready to run out the landing.

Who wouldn't be happy flying this world class site?  The Cox LZ is huge and is a great help to pilots who may get bumped around in the turbulent air as they are landing.

View from just after launch – looking down the HG launch ramp and then on past down the cliff.  The pilot here was airborne the moment his glider came up.  Conditions were right on the edge for PG.  Had we waited any longer, we would have not been able to safely launch.
