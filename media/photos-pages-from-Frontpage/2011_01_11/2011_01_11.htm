 January 11, 2011 – Had Robinson soars the E. Potrillo Mountains, Doña Ana Country, New Mexico

We just LOVE "firsts" around here and pilots who fly this region have plenty of opportunity.  The early hang glider pilots may have flown these mountains – long before paragliding – they were the pioneers.  Thanks to Denae, Doak, Lee Baker, & Lee Boone for their unique firsts!  But Had had to do another – the first paraglider pilot to soar the E Potrillo Mountains (located about 32 miles WNW of El Paso, Texas).  Today was the perfect time to try it.  Winds were east at 10 mph - no gusting.  This is another "Class A" paragliding adventure – it took about an hour to hike from near the bottom to the spine of the mountain chain with 55 lbs.  Because there are no records of how these mountains fly, setup took an hour.  Things have to be perfect when we jump off mountain tops!
