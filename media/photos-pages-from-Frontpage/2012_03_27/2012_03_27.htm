06/06/16

 

March 27 Tuesday – Franklin Mountains – (posted Thursday).  Had Robinson launched from Santa Teresa Airport late in the afternoon and headed towards the Franklins.  It had to be this way as there was not time to make the hike to Lee's Lookout.  Rough air was everywhere along the west side of the Rio Grande Valley...not sure why?  Continuing towards the Franklins and gaining altitude, he was able to cut the engine (peaceful!) and soar the Franklins.  Here are some photos of our dream region.

Below, drifting past "The Elephant" – a rock formation that looks suspiciously like a mammoth that is west of South Mt. Franklin (Radio Peak).  Lift was good but not as strong as other times.

A view of N. Mt. Franklin with S. Mt. Franklin just to the right.  The Tom Mays unit of Franklin Mountains State Park is just visible in the lower right.

The top of N. Mt. Franklin.  The solar powered amateur radio repeater is the tiny white speck.  There is magic lift in front of the peak at this altitude.

A view of Mundy's Gap – the sun is going down.  Most do not realize what a knife-edge much of the top of the Franklin range is.  It is apparent here.  The Organ Mountains are even more jagged and sharp.  We hope to some day be allowed to fly in the them.  Talk about soaring lift!

The gentle slope of the south end of N. Mt. Franklin.  When pilots come the other way, they can slowly soar right up the ridge to the top.  The Transmountain Pass highway separates North and South Mt. Franklin.

It is always spooky flying over the Pass as pilots have had accidents here when conditions were too strong.

The south end of the Franklins.   It's an antenna farm and it was fun soaring right in front of these towers (sorry – no photos!).

Hope you enjoyed the show!
