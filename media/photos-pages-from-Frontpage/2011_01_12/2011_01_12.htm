January 12, 2011 Wednesday – Flying the East Potrillo Mountains

Pilots Bill Cummings, Lee Boone, & Had Robinson set out in the afternoon to see more of the East Potrillo Mountains in south central New Mexico.  Conditions were weak - less than 4 from the east at the LZ.  Please go here to get an idea of where these mountains are.  Lee & Had flew safely and Bill provided important ground support. After soaring the range, everyone took a trip south to the end of the range to check out a launch site that is entirely accessible by 4 wheel. The site needs a little clearing (haircut) of the brush but it should work just fine if the winds are moderate or better.  For a video of Lee's launch, go here.

Our local ace pilot, Lee Boone, sets up the Torrey Paso launch.  (Lee Boone coined this title as the range is a huge Torrey Pines without the crowds.)

Lee pauses near the Torrey Paso launch.  To the left in the distance is the enormous LZ.  It is an easy sled ride from launch.  In laminar conditions, pilots can stay up in light winds – if they stay close to the terrain.  Remember:  We never fly close to the terrain if there is any chance of turbulence!  In the winter, on cloudy days, or late/early in the day the risk of turbulence is negligible.  It is never good to fly when the Jet is overhead.
