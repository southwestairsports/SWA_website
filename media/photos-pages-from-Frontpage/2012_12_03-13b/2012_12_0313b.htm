VALLE DE BRAVO 2012  part ii

December 3 -13 more from Valle de Bravo Part II

A pilot makes the trip from launch to the Peñon (just visible at the top of the photo).  Between launch and the rock it is nothing but sink so pilots must get nearly 1,000' over launch in order to make the journey easily.

Had Robinson preparing to launch from Peñon.  Things must be perfect before leaving the ground in a sport that is often forgiving of mistakes but never often enough.

Bringing her up in perfect form – it is essential that all pilots become masters of inflating their gliders and getting them smoothly overhead, lined up straight in the direction of launch.  Unfortunately, more than half the pilots launching here over the week had poor launching skills which could have gotten them in big trouble if they experienced cascading events.  But happy to say our own little group all had pilots with good skills!

The launch at La Torre above Valle de Bravo.  Beyond the flat area the slope is about 60 degrees with trees on either side.

A view southeast of the Valle LZ.  There are some beautiful estates here.  In less fancy areas, a hacienda on the lake can be had for around $600/month USD and that includes a gardener and a maid.  It is tempting....

Beauty & peace – soaring the ridge behind the pueblo of Valle

A tandem prepares to launch from La Torre.  There will be dozens of tandems in an average afternoon.

 

Below some of my flying buddies, Kip Stone (L) from Maine and Guy-Didier Godat (R) from Washington, D.C. with our constant helper, driver, and friend, Jaime in the middle.  Kip flies a lawn dart (a.k.a. hang glider).

Last of all, I had to include this one – yours truly (Had Robinson) resting in the LZ after a flight.  I hope you will come with us to Valle de Bravo next year.  The little black thing sticking up near my purse is the antenna of a satellite phone.  There are large areas of Mexico in the backcountry (like here) where the cell phone coverage is spotty or non-existent.  (Photo courtesy of John Cummings.)
