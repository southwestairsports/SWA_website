06/06/16

 

April 26 Tuesday – Spring Fling – The Triangle of Doom – Cruising along high over the Central Florida landscape.  At this moment, I am turning in a thermal in order to stay in it.  The skill in this sport is to find lift going up and not fly out of it.  You can't see the thermal but your instruments can tell you via sound when you are near it and in it.  When the thermals are strong, you can feel them – of course.  It is all a learned skill unlike anything else.  The vultures and other soaring birds are experts and we follow and watch them, too.  You can see another glider in the upper right.  He and I are flying together and working the same lift.  He went on to win the day – I took a chance and sank out...as you can see below.

Winds were driving pilots to the northeast when we needed to be going north.  I left good lift and a fellow pilot to see if I could dart across a "blue hole" to the other side and get more on track for goal.  A blue hole is a blue patch of sky which is reputed to be without any lift anywhere whatsoever.  Well, that is the truth.  I landed in the middle of a 100.000 acre cattle ranch and had to walk out with my gear - about 5 miles.  It was an interesting hike out.  This ranch is also known as "The Triangle of Doom" because if you land in it, there is no way for you to be picked up.  In the photo below, I just landed and knew it was going to be a long day with a 55 lb pack.  But what can you do?  It was an unexpected hike as far as the eye could see so why not enjoy it?

When the fields went away, I had to go through stuff like this.  The jungle in Florida is not bad when it is really dry, as it is now.

Occasionally, there were paths through the jungle, as here. It was in the middle 90's that day with 90 percent humidity.

When this stuff is wet, you have to slog through it.  It was dry at the moment – thankfully.

A pine forest with palm weeds growing at the base. 

After the forest, I was out on a plain again.  In the distance, there was rain coming.  I did NOT want to be hiking through this ranch in the rain!  In the far distance, is the road (unseen) behind the tree line.  I am almost there!

I got to the road and was picked up by the retrieve crew, flight/tow babe Brita and my long time friend and fellow pilot, Zak Hargraves.  We are getting fuel for the long journey of picking up the other pilots.  The thunderstorm had come and we are getting rained on.  It was 5 more pilots to go who had landed in the middle of nowhere.  Brita ensures that the pilots are in a good mood after they are picked up.

Flight Kitty was back again that evening as I was working on the scores for the day.  It is all I can do not to resist taking him home.
