October 28 - 30, 2011 Fiesta del Globo Aeroshow Xtremo – Chihuahua City, Chih. Mexico

US PPG pilots Zak Hargraves, Leon Wacker, Matt Watson, and Had Robinson made an easy journey south to Cd. Chihuahua Mexico for one of the biggest air shows in Mexico.  Over 100,000 people attended the event which included balloonists (Mexican & US), skydivers, aero-acrobatic teams, the Mexican Air Force, motocross events, and a powered paragliding demonstration.  We pilots stayed in deluxe accommodations and had all of our expenses covered.  This was provided by: Fiesta del Globo Chihuahua, A.C. (Enrique Elias, President), the State of Chihuahua, and Chihuahua City.  Jose Muñoz was our gracious and helpful host.  It was a wonderful experience demonstrating paragliding for the tens of thousands of visitors that came to the Fiesta.

The Fiesta was held at the west end of a city reservoir, the Presa El Rejón, which is located at the southwest edge of Chihuahua City.  On the south edge of the reservoir is the University of La Salle.

View looking west towards the fairgrounds that hosted the Fiesta del Globo.  La Salle University is just visible to the lower left.  In the distance is the Sierra Madre mountain range.  The Albuquerque group of balloonists were the largest group of pilots at the Fiesta.

The beautiful University of La Salle in Chihuahua City that is located just above the reservoir to the south.  They graciously allowed us to use their soccer field as a PPG airport.  Try getting permission to do this in the States.  Everywhere we landed or flew, we were welcomed by enthusiastic crowds.

These guys carried our equipment, cleared paths through the crowds for us, and did just about anything we needed.  They were so friendly and helpful.

Had with his trusty Miniplane Top 80 paramotor - the smallest and lightest of the powered units.  People would come by and ask us to pose with family and friends for photos.  It's an odd experience to be so popular!

The cooks and servers who served up meals for the pilots at the VIP lounge at the Fiesta.  We never lacked for comida!  On the right is a rare photo of Marilyn Monroe that appeared in Vogue Magazine.  It is on the wall of Jose's hacienda.

This helicopter had the Governor of the State of Chihuahua and his staff on it.  He made an appearance at the Fiesta.

A view of the Fiesta and the general terrain of Chihuahua City.  It is overcast this day - a good thing for us PPG pilots to fly in.

The view southeast from reservoir with La Salle U. visible.  We used the track and soccer field to launch and land our foot launched paragliders as it was higher than the fairgrounds and the winds were less fickle.  The deep inversions that occur in the city every day make the winds near the surface very shifty and changing moment by moment.  Though they are always weak, they greatly affect our ability to foot-launch easily.  The soccer field also has no dirt or rocks.  We like that!  Just visible is a 20' walkway that completely surrounds the reservoir.  At various times, the walkway is jammed with people out for a stroll.  The people of Mexico greatly value their families, open space, the countryside and access to it.

An enormous monument to the traditional family that stands at the east end of the reservoir.

La Salle University. The structure above the walkway near the lake is for a zip line that goes all the way across the water to the other side.  They took it down for the Fiesta as it would have been an invisible hazard to pilots flying low over the water.

Another photo of our helpers. After we do launches requiring a 75 yard run (without getting up in the air), we are exhausted and these guys carry our stuff back for another try.  To the right is another photo of our host, Jose Muñoz and PPG pilot, Had Robinson.

Jose's cousin, Luis Fuentes at one of our many evening parties after flying all day.  He flies a PPG trike.  The VIP lounge (right) was staffed by young camareras who made sure we lacked nothing.
 

Below, the Mexican Army Parachute Team lining up after an aerial display.  They perform much like the US Army Golden Knights.

We all stayed at the Holiday Inn Express in Chihuahua City.  The comfort level and food were first class - quiet and comfortable.

We hope you enjoyed these few photos of the 2011 Fiesta del Globo.  Make plans to join us for next year's celebration!
