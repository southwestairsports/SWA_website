06/06/16

 

 FLY-A-THON - at the sod farm and Hwy 9 - hang gliding and paragliding

Saturday November 13

Saturday was a record for tows and aircraft.  Pilots Robin Hastings (HG), Lee Boone (HG), Denae Nemanic (PG), Lee Baker (PG), Gingher Leyendecker (PG), and Jim MacKenzie (PG) towed up.  Gingher and Jim visited us from Arizona.  Welcome!

Robin did his first ever tow at the sod farm with our hydraulic winch.  Lee Boone towed up as well and practiced safe HG launches and landings. 

Lee Baker and Denae worked on their spot landings.  Denae is "crashing ahead" in her flying skills, perfecting her launch technique and making spot landings (<25').  Matt Watson (PPG student) joined the crowd, helped with launching other pilots and geared himself up for more instruction by doing some kiting.

What a setup!  Hang gliding!  Paragliding!

Left to right: Lee Boone (our first biwingual pilot), Bill Cummings, Robin Hastings (about to launch), and Matt Watson.

Bill Cummings & Robin Hastings confer on the finesse of tow launching - both these HG pilots have immense experience in this field.

Robin prepares to be towed up by EPP's powerful all hydraulic winch.

Lee Baker setting up for a tow.  Note the vast expanse that the sod farm gives pilots who are training.

Denae Nemanic, one of our top women PG pilots, waits for Lee Boone to launch via tow.  Matt Watson (far right) assists.

Robin Hastings gets pulled up for a successful flight.

Left to right:  Jim MacKenzie, pilot Gingher Leyendecker, and Lee Baker.  Gingher and Jim did towed launches for the first time in a long time.  Gingher did a payout tow here on Hwy 9.  The New Mexico State Police have graciously allowed us to litter the highway with ourselves - if we do so in a safe and responsible manner and don't get in the way of anybody else.

Denae Nemanic cruises over the desert west of El Paso from a flight that topped about 8,000' - all from a payout tow.  This was her first high altitude flight -- is she excited and charged for more?  One could say that....

Denae comes in for a landing at dusk-thirty.  Note how she has her landing gear down!

Ace pilot and native, Lee Boone, congratulates Denae on a successful and safe high altitude flight.  It was really getting dark and the lights on our vehicles were easy for her to see.  She landed dead in the middle of the road right at where we parked.  GOOD JOB, DENAE!
