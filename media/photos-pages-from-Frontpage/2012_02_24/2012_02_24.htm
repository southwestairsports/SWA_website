06/06/16

 

February 24 Friday - Gangbusters Part II in the Potrillo Mountains – Paragliding is like surfing a bit: You get out in the water.  You may be early but what a disappointment if you are too late!  Better to wait around at "launch" than to watch a dream wave come along while you are on the beach.  Well, pilots Lee Boone, Bele Christine, Had Robinson, and the KFOX Channel 14 camera crew barely got out to the East Potrillo Mountains in time – that's when I (Had) thought, "We are like surfers and we almost missed the wave!"

Below, ace reporter, Bernadette Flores of KFOX News; intrepid pilot adventurer, Lee Boone; Bele Christine (not pictured here), and wanna-be record-setter, paragliding instructor, and all around "crank", Had Robinson just before our flight in the Potrillo's.  How do we have any more fun than this:  Flying places no man has ever flown before???

Lee Boone being interviewed by the crew from KFOX.  In the background:  The East Potrillo Mountains.

Bele and Had – we who paraglide are all best friends.  Bele did not fly today but she helped us launch.  For this the active pilots are extremely thankful as safety always is better with help.

Lee & Had on their way to Torrey Paso launch.  The packs weigh 55 lbs. each.

Both pilots had perfect launches from Torrey Paso.  Below is Had's launch.  Note the symmetrical shape of the glider which is fully inflated.  When setting up on the ground, careful inspection of the lines is a must.  A single line can snag on a bush or a sharp rock and spoil the launch which can also damage the glider.  The harness looks very large but is, in fact, a ram inflatable air bag – in case of mishap.  We launched safely, soared the bowl in front of Torrey Paso launch, and then landed.  This was a "hike-n-fly" event and was worth 20 minute trip to launch.

Had is heading north along the crest of the range.  It was the end of the day and the thermals – sniff – were going away.

Below is our friendly barrel cactus.  It looks frightening but, by cactus standards, is more passive.  The spines are not barbed on the ends nor do they contain poison unlike the prickly pear and the cholla which both are nasty if you just touch them.  The yellow pods at the top of the cactus are mature seed pods each of which has hundreds of small black seeds about 1/3 the size of a grain of rice.  Bele has placed her foot at the base to give an idea of its size.

Lee and Bele sitting at the LZ at the base of the range.  Lee is sitting in his flying harness enjoying the quiet late afternoon miles from nowhere.

Our signature mark of soaring pilots in the vicinity: The windsock.  This one will show air moving just a fraction of a mile per hour.  We always want to launch or land into the wind.

Hope you enjoyed the show!
