July 21 Saturday – Trip down the Rio Grande through El Paso, Texas/Juarez, Mexico

Adventurers Glenn Tupper and Had Robinson wanted to set another first for powered ultralights in the world:  A trip down the Rio Grande right on the border of the two cities of El Paso, TX USA and Cd. Juarez, Chih. Mexico. An outstanding YouTube video of the trip highlights created by Glenn can be seen here. To see more of Glenn's outstanding aerial photography and videos, go here.  Launching from the Gardiner Turf farms in Santa Teresa, NM we headed southeast across the desert.  (Note: U.S. border and air authorities were properly notified and gave us a "heads up" for the flight.)

The busy Stanton Street bridge over the concrete gully know as the Rio Grande river.  Thousands of Mexican pedestrians are waiting in line to apply for immigration status largely to avoid the violence in the world's most violent city.  We did not have any bullet holes in our wings (we would shoot back, anyway).

Glenn heading southeast from the towards the border.  One of the largest solar cell farms in the nation is in the background.  It is located in Santa Teresa, NM.  Glenn is flying the Paramania Fusion powered with Parajet Polini 200.

Our view of the Rio Grande (before it goes into irrigation ditches and disappears).  This view is southeast.

Passing by Mt. Cristo Rey where a huge Crucifix stands at the very top of the mountain.  View is south and much of Juarez is in the background.

The ASARCO smokestack – when it was built it was the tallest structure in the world.  Engineers designed it so that effluent would be emitted in the air above the still air of the deep inversion that forms in the region every night (it is about 1,000' thick).  In this way, the smoke would enter the moving air above the inversion instead of filling the city with smoke every night.  Of course, residents would not be protected from the massive amounts of lead dust which would still waft their way down – even through the inversion,  We would have flown closer but we hate making the 6 o'clock news for any reason....

Downtown El Paso with Interstate Hwy 10 running right along the border.

We are both exactly over the border with Mexico in this photo.

Another photo of the jammed Stanton Street bridge.  Juarez is one of the top examples in the world of this cliché, "When guns are outlawed, only outlaws will have guns."  Ordinary Mexican citizens armed with 12 gauge shotguns would have likely ended this pandemonium a decade ago.  El Paso, on the other hand, is the safest city of its size in the U.S.  If your town was being shot up by the battle between violent thugs and government troops, what would you do?

A one of a kind photo of downtown El Paso from its southern border.  The ASARCO stack is visible in the distance.

The mighty Rio Grande river (the dry gulch between the bushes).  All the water in the river runs through a canal east of here to farms in the Rio Grande valley southeast of the city.  This region has become extremely dry in recent years.

I, the wingman, flying along with Glenn Tupper as we cruise up the border to the northwest on our return trip.  The Mexican police (or whoever poses as them) followed us along to the left for a while.  We often trade wingman positions as we had no idea what the Mexican authorities would do.  If we had a problem (being shot at), we would immediately land on the U.S. side of the river and take refuge with the U.S. Border Patrol who are everywhere.

I hope you enjoyed the show!  How can a guy not smile after having so much fun and a safe landing?  Glenn Tupper is one of the top PPG adventurers on planet earth and I hope you will visit his website.  We see the world from a perspective not shared by any other aircraft.  Our problem?  Glenn and I egg each other on....  – Had Robinson
