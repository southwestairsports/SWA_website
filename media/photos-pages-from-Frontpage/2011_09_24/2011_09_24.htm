06/06/16

 

September 24 Saturday – A Magic Day at Lee's Lookout in the Franklins – PG Pilots Chris Nystuen, Lee Boone, Doak Hoover, and Had Robinson all agreed that it was worth the hike to Lee's Lookout today as the forecast was for a westerly flow right up to altitude.  We have had month after month this year of the most frustrating winds for our PG sites and we pilots were just frantic to have some smooth southwesterly flow into our region.  Finally, we had some good southwest air in the afternoon and we were able to fly!

L-R Chris, Doak, and Lee prepping for launch from one of the most challenging launch sites in the US.

Lee looking at the air before committing to launch.  Once a pilot commits, he cannot change his mind.  Unless he goes straight up, he will follow the ridge all the way out to the front of the range where there are many active thermals.

Below, Lee Boone climbing out after launch.  There is nothing like a successful bench up from a mountain site.  Skill is required!

Telephoto shot of Lee above the ridge behind launch.  No - he is not about to fly into some power lines.  They are far in front and down from his location.

Lee flying over launch (telephoto shot).

Doak getting ready to launch.  We hope to cut some of the edge off launch to make things easier to setup.

Doak climbing out after launch.  He later flew to S. Mount Franklin, got high, and then went north, crossing the Dragon's Mouth, and soared to 9,000' MSL above N. Mount Franklin – a awesome flight that no one else did that day.  Good job, Doak!

Lee Boone soaring way above S. Mount Franklin.

Until next time...fly safely!
