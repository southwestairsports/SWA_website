Pilot Lee Baker:  First to thermal away from a tow at the sod farm
Saturday, August 28, 2010

Conditions were supreme in our region.  A low pressure system and plenty of moisture in the air made conditions optimal for thermalling:  Air was buoyant everywhere.  Thermals were big, wide, and strong.  We only have days like this a few days a year.  With the help of instructor Had Robinson, Lee was able to snag a big thermal off tow at approximately 700' AGL and go to near cloud base at 7,600' MSL

Tanner Sanderson and pilot Denae Nemanic assist Lee at the start of the tow.  Lee will inflate his wing while facing it, then turn forward and go!

Lee begins his launch run.  Often, the glider may inflate off center and the pilot must immediately center his wing overhead, even while still running -- as Lee does here.

The clouds above launch are dense -- lift is everywhere.  Lee is relaxed while being pulled to altitude.

Five minutes after the tow was complete, Lee was thermalling away and enjoying the incomparable experience of being right next to a cloud.  Below, he is just south of the very strong lift in a developing cloud.  It is good to stay at the edge in order to ensure that one might not be sucked into it.

As thermals and cloud formation were becoming more intense and hazardous for newer pilots, Lee came down -- a 15 minute task when the air is going up everywhere.

Pilot Lee Baker and ground crew Denae, Tirzah Robinson, and Tanner are all smiles - "Having more fun than a guy is allowed to have...."  Congrats Lee for a job well done!
