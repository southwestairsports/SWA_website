06/06/16

 

July 27 Thursday – UP, UP, & AWAY on Highway 9 –   P3 student pilot Brad Gray had the best flights ever in some of the best air we ever have in the region.  Conditions in the afternoon were slightly unstable in our moist desert air so thermals were big and organized.  Launching under tow towards the east around 4PM, Brad was treated to his first thermalling experiences.  The air was so good that he did not even need a variometer (he forgot to fly with it, anyway). He was able to get near cloud base and fly cross country for over an hour.  CONGRATS BRAD!!!

55 miles of road for towing!  View here is west – why we live and fly in the southwest!  No traffic – no crowds – no obstructions – endless sky!

Brad prepares to do a reverse inflation and then launch under tow.  The orange drogue parachute can be seen on his lower left.  The drogue helps us retrieve the two miles of towline by keeping the line in the air.
