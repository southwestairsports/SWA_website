Denae Nemanic becomes first woman to launch from the summit of N. Mt. Franklin, Franklin Mountains State Park, Texas

December 28 - Tuesday was an historic day for us in our region as Denae Nemanic became the first woman to launch a paraglider from the top of N. Mt. Franklin.  Denae, instructor Had Robinson, pilot Lee Boone, and Dominic & his dad, Rafael Padilla, all made the 2.5 hour hike to the top.  KVIA Channel 7 News covered the event as well.  For more on this story go here - we are still waiting for more photos form the many photographers who were at launch.

Gerardo Najera of KVIA Channel 7 News interviews our heroine.

Dominic and his dad, Rafael pose at the entrance of Tom Mays with N. Mt. Franklin in the background.  They provided essential help as Sherpas for carrying gear to the summit.

Denae at the upper parking lot where all hikers must begin.

Local guide and photographer, Ken Steiner (left), and pilot Lee Boone discuss the Franklin Mountains at Mundy’s Gap on the way to the top.  Ken was leading a large group of hikers to the summit of N. Mt. Franklin.  They were able to see the pilots fly away from the summit.

At the summit, preparing to launch.

Denae and friend Dave just after her successful flight.  She landed on the access road in Tom Mays.  It took 20 minutes for her to fly from the summit.  Winds were beginning to build and it was good for less experienced pilots to come down.
