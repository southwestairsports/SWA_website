Part II - December 28, 2010 (for Part I, go here)

 

Denae Nemanic becomes first woman to launch from the summit of N. Mt. Franklin, Franklin Mountains State Park, Texas

 

Here are more outstanding photos taken by local mountain guide and photographer Ken Steiner.  He was taking a group of hikers through the Franklins that day and we happened to be there!
 

(L-R) Rafael Padilla, Denae Nemanic, Had Robinson, Lee Boone, & Dominic Padilla

 

It was 2.5 hours to the top!  About 2,500’ of vertical climb.

 

About 500’ below the summit looking north.
\

 

Rafael and Lee near the summit.  We are far above the inversion here but the air was still full of moisture from the storms we have had in the region.

 

Dominic and Had laying out Denae’s glider at the summit -- 7200’

 

Lee Boone getting ready – he launched first.  The object to the right is the amateur radio repeater tower at 146.88 mhz – it is a solar powered repeater that, after 30 years or so, is still the best in the region – pilots who are ham radio operators can hit this repeater from almost anywhere within 100 miles.

 

The three pilots discussing the flight – we always have a plan!  The Organ Mountains east of Las Cruces, NM, are visible in the distance.

 

Lee Boone launching his Niviuk Koyot


 


 

 

Denae getting ready to go


 


 


 

 

Denae (L) and Lee (R) high above the Franklins.  They are both in the “sweet spot” south and west of the summit of N. Mt. Franklin.  The air there is always going up….


 

Lee (L) and Denae (R) – view is southwest.  Mexico is in the background.


 

Our heroine, Denae, in front of the summit in here APCO Karma SP


 

Had Robinson launching in his Gin Zulu glider.  It was great to have Rafael and Dominic to help us launch as well as carry gear – thanks guys!
