 Kilbourne Hole maar - soaring in the late afternoon

Sunday November 14 - for a 1 minute video of this flight go here.

As if the day before was not enough for pilots in the southwest, we went out to our dream soaring site, Kilbourne Hole maar.

The Jet overhead, even though 5 miles up, still rocks and rolls the atmosphere down low for us ultra lights (or "leaves" really). I (Had Robinson) had not flown in over 4 months (recovering from a prop strike done by a student) so it was "take it easy". Conditions at the east launch were a steady 15 - 19 mph from the west so I waited until almost 4PM to launch. What can we say: Smooth as glass air while soaring a maar in a region that is off the grid. We can be so thankful to have such a safe and beautiful site in our great New Mexican southwest!

The view south with the east launch in view.  Our truck is just visible.  It's almost 400' to the bottom.  The air going up at rim of the crater is smooth as glass.  There are, however, spots along the rim where the air is focused by the black basalt cliffs that line the maar.

View northwest from above launch.  The maar is huge, one of the larger ones in the world.  It is also one of the only places on earth has a rock called "green peridot."  The bail-out LZ is easily visible here - it is about 1/4 mile across and is composed of tall grass.

This photo looks east and is directly in front of launch.  Winds can vary over 120 degrees and it is still safe to launch here.  Flying a maar is an unforgettable experience.
