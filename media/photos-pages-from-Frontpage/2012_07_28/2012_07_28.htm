July 28 Saturday – Brad Gray goes Cross Country beating the regional record – What was to be just another towing day turned into epic cross country.  With coaching from his instructor, Had Robinson, the P2 pilot went west along Hwy 9 for nearly 44 miles from near the Santa Teresa station of the U.S. Border patrol to the east side of Columbus, New Mexico.

Brad getting towed up over (1) mile.  Southwest Paragliding's winch has over 10,000' of line so pilots can get HIGH!  Hwy 9 is just the place to tow because there are no crossroads nor any lines crossing the road all the way to Columbus.  The cloud street is visible to the right of the photo.  It went for at least 50 miles – probably over 200 miles and way into Arizona.  As a new pilot, Had gave Brad some help getting high enough to get in the really smooth and big lift.

Brad is right next to the cloud in the center of the photo below.  See if you can find him.  It will take a lens to see him.  If you give up, go to the next photo below....

Clouds are really huge, especially next to a small aircraft like a paraglider.

Brad is somewhere "out there" down Hwy 9.  We lost visual with him but were able to keep in radio contact. If there had not been overdevelopment he could have likely beaten the state XC record of 250 miles or so.  It was a one in a hundred days for our region of buoyant air and strong east winds – perfect for a long XC trip.
