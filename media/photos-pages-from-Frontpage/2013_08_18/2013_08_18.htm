Woods Hole Massachusetts – A Powered Paragliding Paradise on the Ocean

A beautiful mix of bays, harbors, fresh and saltwater ponds and marshes, and islands – this sparsely populated region can provide a powered paraglider pilot with stunning sea and landscapes.  Woods Hole is situated on the southern tip on Cape Cod.  The hole itself is a small but important passage between Vineyard Sound and Buzzards Bay and has a strong tidal current that reach in excess of (7) knots.  The narrow passage is one of the most dangerous on the east coast with many boats going aground on the rocks each year.

Looking east along Tarpaulin Cove – one of the safest and most protected coves along the Elizabeth Islands.  This is because of its deep water and shelter from the storms coming from the southwest to the northeast.  It used be the depot where U.S. mail was exchanged with great ships that were in the region.  These days it is just an infrequent tourist destination.
