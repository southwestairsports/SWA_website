 Epic Conditions in the Franklin Mountains
June 2, 2009

Pilots Zak Hargraves & Had Robinson fly the Franklin Mountains in Epic Conditions.  There is a 3 1/2 minute video of the flight here as well as in our general video section.

View of South Mt. Franklin (“Radio Peak”) looking SE.


Looking down at the Coronado Country Club to the SW


The top of our highest peak in the region – N. Mt. Franklin – near dusk. The gray/red thing is my harness. Had is approaching 8,000’ and still going up from massive ridge lift that is generated in the Franklins. Why is this? This single ridge of mountains is geographically isolated so air can come in from the east or west undisturbed by other mountain chains.  There is lift over a mile out in front at altitude (+1,000 AGL). The air is soft and smooth.  The road and observation area cut during WWII is clearly visible.


Had Robinson is about a half mile in front of N. Mt. Franklin at 8,000’ – and still going up. To descend from this height takes time so – sniff – we pilots had to take measures to come down so we wouldn’t be attempting to land (and fly) in the dark. Not only is it illegal for us, it’s somewhat dangerous….



If you look closely, you can see Zak’s glider 2,000’ below near a ridge to the west and front of N. Mt. Franklin.. He is going up – I’m going down. I still have about 20 minutes to go to get to the LZ.


Moonrise at 8,000’ in Franklin Mountains State Park


Zak ascending in the “glass off” above the Rio Grande Valley. He is a few miles out in front of the Franklins. It’s after dusk and we have ½ hour before we must land. This is the magic time for flying a hang glider or a paraglider in ultra-smooth conditions.


It’s getting dark and Zak (pictured here) and I must come down! Visible in the far distance are the Little Florida Mountains south of Deming, New Mexico. The closer mountains are the West Potrillo Mountains – we also fly in them!


Hope you enjoyed the show!
