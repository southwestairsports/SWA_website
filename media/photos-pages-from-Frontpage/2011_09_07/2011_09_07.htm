A view of the newly completed solar power farm near the US - Mexico border in Santa Teresa, NM.  This array is about 10 acres in size. Each row of solar cells follows the sun as it travels across the sky.  This would be a great way to turn sunlight into electricity but it is still not cost effective – not even close. Each separate row of panels (about 120 sq. ft.) costs around $8,000.  The entire installation probably cost around $60 - $80 million.  Solar power only works when the sun is shining brightly so those who only have solar power have to have batteries to store power during the night.  Naturally, this installation (required to be built by law) is heavily subsidized by taxpayers in New Mexico and the US.  It will never pay for itself as the panels will wear out long before and will have to be replaced.  Thanks to over-zealous environmentalists and the cozy relationship between big business and legislatures in our country, we are forced to enrich the companies that make this stuff and those who install them.  The same is true with the wind farms.  We are going bankrupt because people have little sense and continue to elect rascals who dip their hands into our pockets.  Voters let them get away with this because they think there is no end to the money supply.  It's all crazy.  These cells are probably made in China.  Don't you wish you could be among the owners of the company that has that kind of clout with the legislatures?  I wonder what the lobbyist made who pushed this through?
