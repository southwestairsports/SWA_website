06/06/16

 

September 12 Wednesday PM – Kilbourne Hole Maar – Air to die for!  In the afternoon, pilots Wade Gonder, Brad Gray, Brad's dad, Nelson Gray, and host/instructor Had Robinson traveled out to Kilbourne Hole.  The forecasts the day before were for west winds in the 10 mph range at Deming (west of Kilbourne) which is perfect for PG soaring at the Hole – and so it was!  When Kilbourne is ON, the natural beauty of the Maar, its isolation from the rest of the planet, and the proximity of the mountains to the west all make this one of the top soaring sites in the southwest.

Below L-R, Wade preparing to launch from the east rim of the maar.  Brad & Nelson stand by to assist, if needed.

Off Wade goes – north up the east rim

The unique shape of the maar's rim allows an amazing gradient effect that allows a pilot to finesse his way back to launch and park over it at various heights.  Here, Wade is just 20' above the launch area.

After a tour of the rim, Wade does a top landing northeast of launch.  If you don't top land, it's a 45 minute hike out of the bottom of the maar.

Brad carefully checks his canopy prior to committing to launch.  Launching is a relaxed task: The pilot moves forward to the edge of the inside rim, a very gradual slope down.  The air vector changes smoothly from more horizontal to more vertical.  Each step forward increases the lift on the canopy.  If the pilot changes his mind, he need only back up a few steps and the lift decreases.  It's the magic of Kilbourne.

Brad heads up north along the rim – slowly going up.

Wade did another launch to join Brad in the air.  The hills visible above the rim are about 3 miles away and are some small volcanic cones southeast of Aden Crater.

Brad goes to the top of the lift at Kilbourne and makes a turn.  He is enjoying the buoyant and calm air of the maar late in the day.

Brad (L) and Wade (R) enjoying floating above the world in the gathering dusk.  It's an ethereal experience soaring such a place like the eagles and vultures – it cannot be explained.

Brad traverses the rim back to launch.  The sun has gone down.

Wade readying to come down before it gets really dark – the maar is off the grid and is in dark sky country.  In the distance are the West Potrillo Mountains.

Wade top landing right at launch.  Most sites do not allow this luxury – another jewel of the maar.

Brad came down just before all light was gone.  What a view of the world and dusk in the desert!  A huge storm cell is visible to the north near Silver City and the Gila Wilderness.  During this time of transition, the world is full of stark contrasts.  The West Potrillo Mountains, the Little Florida Mountains, Mt. Riley, Mt. Cox, Mt. No-Name, Aden Crater, Hunt's Hole, and the lava fields are all visible – to a pilot parked over the rim of Kilbourne Hole.

I hope you enjoyed the show!  -  Had
