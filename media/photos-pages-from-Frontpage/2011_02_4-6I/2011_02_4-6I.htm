 Salton Sea PPG fly-in February 4-6, 2001 Part I

Hundreds of PPG pilots have gathered to fly the balmy skies of the Salton Sea of south central California. It right on the west coast of the Sea about half way up in the town of Salton City.  Because of the huge body of water, the air is stable and pleasant (as well as safe) for powered paragliders. Here are some photos of the event.

It is good news that PPG'ers are figuring out that kiting a paraglider is a skill worth learning well.  My heart glowed to see these pilots honing their skills.

View of the Salton Sea launch area.  Just to the right is a huge RV park where I am parked.  It has a geothermal hot spring spa and complete facilities, including a restaurant that is open for breakfast and special occasions.

View of the Salton Sea looking NNW.  It is a lonely area that was the subject of development a few decades ago.  Lack of water, extreme heat in the summer, and the smell of the sea (it is getting more saline with a lot of dead fish) are the likely reasons this area is relatively undeveloped.  Today (Saturday) I plan to launch midday, go inland, and thermal away to some good altitude!

Typical dunes landscape about 7 miles north of launch – I had just landed after a nearly 2 hour flight.  I went into the winds aloft to test the air and the forecasts.  While 3,500' and below has still air (caused by the warming of such air by the Sea), the air above this altitude was moving:  25+ mph.  Going through the layers of air was interesting (somewhat turbulent).  As I went up, I slowed down and wound up flying backwards.  It was easy to see the huge bubble of air over the Sea from above where the air was very clear.  Matter of different temperatures does not tend to mix, as here.  The winds aloft were from the north and split around the Sea as well as going over it.  Us PG pilots love to fly high.  However, 4,000' AGL is not much....
