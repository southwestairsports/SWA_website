Flying Sites in the Southwest

Read the introduction to the sites in our region.  We can provide guide services to any of these sites.  The Rio Grande Soaring Association can, on an informal basis, provide limited help for pilots who wish to visit these sites.  There are plenty of places to stay and eat along Interstate 10, particularly around Exit 11.  Camping can be done easily west of town in New Mexico on BLM land.

There are other outstanding sites that we fly but, because they are sensitive, the information is not listed here.

Most of the U.S. is relentlessly hostile to adventure sports, like hang gliding and paragliding.  The administrators of public lands in virtually all other countries welcome our sport.  As one of our pilots noted, the "look-but-don't-touch" people run the show here.  Contrast this with Europe.  Switzerland, for example, even put an image of a guy paragliding on their 50 Franc note.  There are exceptions.  We are thankful that the Texas Parks and Wildlife Department has opened the doors of Texas Parks to us.  Other public land administrators should do the same.
