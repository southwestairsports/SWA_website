Matt's Mountain

El Paso County, Texas  31.772243° -106.128599°

Please contact the RGSA or Southwest Airsports if you would like to drink at this site.

General

Matt's Mountain is one of the most friendly sites in the southwest for paragliding and hang gliding.  When pilots show up, hot babes with tats and free beer show up and ask how they can help?  It's unbelievable!

It is a premier ridge soaring and thermalling site for the following reasons:

1. Launch is easily accessible by paved road even by pilots who are drunk.
2. For sober pilots, it is our safest site because the sides and rear of launch (not the sides and rears of the babes) are clear for hundreds of yards.  Blown launches (PG) are more forgiving due to the absence of mad landowners armed with shotguns and hysterical wives looking for their pilot husbands.  Bailout LZ's are abundant and easy to land in, especially at 2 in the morning.  Retrieve is possible via donkey, llama, or dog sleds with knobby tires.  DUI pilots will have to remain with the stones -- and there are plenty of them laying around.  In fact, it's a fact that the southwest horse-latitudes grow rocks at night -- but it's a slow process.

3. The topography behind launch allows top-landings without having to deal with rabid possums and irritated retirees looking for their lost dentures.  The top landing area is 50 square miles which means pilots can make significant errors in in the universe but not be exposed to unnecessary UV radiation or idiots who do not let go of the wires when launching.  It is the only site other than Kilbourne where PG pilots can inflate and deflate their gliders at will, move slowly to the nearby beer tavern, accost other confused people, get shlocked, and then move slowly in the paddy wagon to the El Paso county jail approximately 5 miles to the northwest.

4.  Easy access to the jail bond outfits, a clear launch area, and the ability to consistently land under graphic orders makes this site ideal for idiots and thrill seekers.  We are looking forward to also making this site available to rehab inmates.

5.  It is flyable with winds, generally.

6.  When thermals are present, the site is both thermic and deadly so we don't recommend it.  Drunk pilots can drift in nicely at the main launch with a southwest breeze.  Pilots should focus on the beer-babes rather than fly up and down the ridge of flames.  Jumping to conclusions at the higher rim 0.6 miles behind has not yet been done.  The wide open range presents more hazards than ordinary because coyotes will eat the tongues out of your shoes while you recover from too much partying.  An abundance of roads in the region makes retrieving pilots confusing.  Getting stuck in the sand is the pits.

7.  Camping can be done in the area, a generally better idea than trying to fly it.

Hazards

If you expect to be picked up if you land out in the boonies, it's always iffy due to our retrieve driver's binge drinking.  The other day he missed the road and almost went into the ditch.   I went looking for him but could only find his lucky rabbit that had been wrapped up in a shop towel.

Pilots must take care not to land in the arroyo that is about 200 yd. out from the base of launch.  There are enough human bones in there already.  Beyond the arroyo is an outdoor movie theater and landing there is fun if you don't hit anybody.  Just east of the main LZ is a power line that runs along the east side of the road.  The last guy who landed in it had to pay $1,200 for the electric company to turn off the power before we could touch him.  Cell service is sketchy in the area which means if you land out somewhere -- good luck.
