Powered Paragliding Instruction – Tuition Schedule

The PPG-1 & PPG-2 courses include all gear necessary for training except the paramotor.  As the pilot progresses through the PPG1 course, both he and the instructor will work together to make the best decision on overall equipment needs.

Here are the details on the USPPA ratings and requirements.

Discovery training flight – $225

For anyone interested in seeing what paragliding is like, we can suggest a Discovery training flight which consists of some basic instruction at our shop and a solo flight at the sod farms where we train.

Before your flight, you will spend an hour or so at our shop and receive some abbreviated flight school instruction in a simulator to get some of the basics down of flying a paraglider.  This can be in the late afternoon, early evening, or when you are available.  You will also pay the tuition and complete the paperwork at this time.  Your instructor will meet with you at our training area near Santa Teresa, NM (just west of El Paso, TX).  You will be fitted with a glider, harness, helmet, and radio and undergo some more instruction in how to fly a paraglider.  Launching a paraglider is similar to launching an ordinary kite flown by children.  You will then learn how to inflate your glider by running forward.  When the instructor determines that you are reasonably comfortable doing a simple inflation, he will hook you up to short line and manually pull you while you are running forward with the inflated glider overhead.  This will give you an idea what it is like to be pulled by our hydraulic winch.  If all goes well, you will be connected to the winch line and do a short "bunny hop" run.  If that goes well, we will pull you up with the winch.  Your final height will be anywhere from 500' - 1,000'.  Once aloft, the instructor will ask you to disconnect from the tow line, do a turn or two and land.  Here is a video of what the launch looks like.

All persons taking a Discovery training flight must meet our mental and physical requirements.

Tandem training flights

We do PPG foot-launched tandem flights at this time at no charge in celebration of our PASA certification (as time permits).  Please see our tandem operations page for more information.  The weight limit for persons wanting to fly tandem is 190 lbs., not 200 lbs. as mentioned in the physical and mental requirements section.

PPG-1 Basic Training – $490 3 days

The PPG-1 Basic Training will cover the fundamentals of powered paragliding. You will learn to inflate the paraglider and balance the kite overhead. Training includes covering the terminology, operation and preflight of the motor unit. We will practice the launch sequence in a simulator, and you will experience your first flights in a paraglider (without a paramotor attached). The progress of each student is individual and advancement towards powered flight and/or certification depends on the student's ability to master the necessary skills.  Here is info on the USPPA ratings and requirements.

 Paramotoring requires more training than regular paragliding because of the added weight and complexity of the paramotor.  Handling of the glider is significantly more difficult so a pilot must have a good set of basic skills to operate a paramotor safely. We want all pilots to become confident with their skills so they can have a lifetime of safe flight. Your instructor will help you make the right decisions concerning things like the weather and equipment purchases. The first phase of PPG-1 Basic Training includes the use of our glider, helmet, radio, and harness.  Students must purchase their own paramotor, at least, in order to complete the final phase of the PPG-1 course.

If you decide to continue your training to the PPG-2 level and above, you will receive a full credit for the cost of your PPG-1 training towards additional training.  

PPG-2 Beginner Training – $1,990 5-12 Days

(If you completed the PPG-1 course with us, you will receive a full credit for the PPG-1 tuition paid.)

Master the fundamentals of flight in a powered paraglider.  This course will take a new student through both PPG-1 (if he has not already completed it) and PPG-2 certification by a USPPA certified instructor.  It includes a complete overview of paramotoring and powered paragliding. Upon completion of this course, students are ready for extended paramotoring flights with their own powered paraglider. This is the minimum certification level that allows PPG pilots to fly without the direct supervision of an instructor.  If you are considering any PPG certification, we highly recommend that you purchase the DVD "Risk and Reward" and watch it many times.  It is available from us.  It will give you a clear presentation of what PPG is all about.

Students will:

  • Understand the paramotor power pack: assembly, disassembly, preflight, starting, throttle control, power cutoff, fuel/oil mixing, maintenance, torque and thrust effects, special dangers, transporting and storage.
  • Earn both the PPG-1 and PPG-2 certifications.
  • Learn to control the wing through turns and mild turbulence.
  • Begin to understand meteorology and its importance for safe and fun flights here in the desert southwest. Pilots who learn to fly in the mountainous desert of west Texas and New Mexico can fly anywhere.

Training Includes:

  • USPPA Syllabus study materials (does not include the PPG Bible textbook which must be purchased separately)
  • Computer based log book
  • Ground school
  • Unlimited hands-on instruction at the training site for your first powered flights.

Training for PPG2 does NOT include flying equipment such as a glider, paramotor, or accessories.  You can purchase this equipment from us prior to training or during the last part of your PPG1 training.

PPG-3 Intermediate Training – $450 3 days

Pilots training for PPG3 must be able to successfully complete a cross-country trip.  Please contact us for more information on this advanced certification. 

Crossover Training (PG to PPG or PPG to PG) – $450 1-2 days

Students who already have a P2 or higher rating will need less instruction and time to become PPG-2 and vice versa.  Pilots may choose to earn USHPA Certification. This will allow the pilot to free-fly a paraglider at many regulated sites.

Additional Notes

We are always careful to heed all FAA and local regulations concerning ultralight aircraft.  Irresponsible and selfish pilots travel about the country spoiling the reputation of all ultralight pilots by engaging in dangerous, annoying, and reckless behavior.  By the time the authorities and the locals are on top of things -- they are long gone.  Then, when you show up to fly, you face nothing but offended and angry people.

If you see or know pilots like this, please contact us and we will do what we can to stop it.
